# Technical Brief for Website Structure and UX Logic

**Project:** Nordic Solutions corporate website  
**Website type:** Corporate presentation / business card website  
**Priority:** Structure, logic, interface, and user flow

> **Important note:** The website must remain neutral, discreet, and bank-friendly. It must not look like a direct sales platform for military or sensitive products.

---

## 1. Main Objective of the Website

The new website must serve as a **corporate representation website**, not as a product catalog and not as an aggressive sales landing page.

Its purpose is to:

- present the company in a professional, modern, and credible way;
- create a strong first impression;
- support trust during bank onboarding, due diligence, partner review, and first contact with potential clients;
- provide a clear and elegant company profile;
- encourage visitors to make contact, without disclosing sensitive operational details publicly.

The website should feel like the website of a serious international company involved in complex projects, partnerships, and advisory / implementation support.

---

## 2. General Positioning Requirement

The website must look:

- modern;
- premium;
- corporate;
- international;
- discreet;
- structured;
- easy to understand.

It must **not** look like:

- a product marketplace;
- a military e-commerce site;
- a defense hardware catalog;
- an empty template website;
- a startup landing page built only on vague slogans.

The required balance is:

**clean + credible + neutral + substantial**

---

## 3. Core UX Problem to Solve

The current new website looks visually better than the old one, but it still lacks:

- clear page logic;
- structured content flow;
- information hierarchy;
- strong navigation logic;
- user direction;
- a proper call-to-action path.

At the moment, it feels visually modern, but too abstract and somewhat empty.

The revised version must fix this by introducing a clear narrative and business flow.

---

## 4. Required Website Structure

### A. Header / Top Navigation

The header should be clean, minimal, and informative.

**Recommended menu structure:**

- Home
- About
- Services
- Approach
- Why Nordic
- Contact

**Alternative shorter version:**

- Home
- About
- Capabilities
- Approach
- Contact

**Header requirements:**

- sticky header on scroll;
- very clean navigation;
- visible Contact button;
- simple and polished mobile version;
- no overloaded dropdowns unless absolutely necessary.

---

### B. Hero Section

This section is critical.

#### Purpose

The first screen must immediately communicate:

- this is a serious international company;
- the company works on complex projects;
- the company is structured and credible;
- the visitor understands the tone within 3–5 seconds.

#### Hero must contain

1. Clear headline
2. Short supporting text
3. Primary CTA button
4. Optional secondary CTA
5. Strong visual balance and spacing

#### Important rules

The hero must not be vague, poetic, or empty.  
It must also not be too explicit about sensitive sectors.

#### Positioning direction

Headline and subheadline should position the company as operating in:

- strategic projects;
- industrial partnerships;
- technology integration;
- complex international execution.

#### CTA examples

- Contact Us
- Request Introduction
- Get in Touch

The hero must feel controlled, premium, and confident.

---

### C. About Section

This block should appear early in the page structure.

#### Purpose

To explain who the company is in a compact and credible way.

#### Must include

- short company introduction;
- international positioning;
- focus on long-term partnerships;
- focus on execution, coordination, and structured project support.

#### UX requirement

This section should be visually broken into readable blocks, not one large paragraph.

**Suggested layout:**

- short text on the left;
- supporting key points, highlights, or trust indicators on the right.

---

### D. Services / Capabilities Section

This section is necessary, even if the website remains discreet.

#### Important rule

Do **not** make it product-specific or sensitive.

This section should present **3–5 broad corporate capabilities**.

**Example structure:**

- Strategic Project Development
- Industrial Partnerships
- Technology Integration
- Supply Chain Structuring
- Implementation Support

#### UX requirement

Display this section as structured cards or clean content blocks.

Each card should include:

- title;
- short description;
- optional subtle icon or visual marker.

This section should make the company look real and operational, while remaining neutral and discreet.

---

### E. Approach / How We Work Section

This section is currently missing in the logic and should be added.

#### Purpose

To show that the company has a process and operational discipline.

#### Why it matters

This increases credibility with:

- banks;
- partners;
- institutional stakeholders.

#### Recommended structure

A 4-step or 5-step process, for example:

1. Assessment
2. Structuring
3. Partner Coordination
4. Implementation Support
5. Ongoing Engagement

#### UX requirement

This section should be visually clear and easy to scan.

- horizontal on desktop;
- vertical on mobile.

This section is important because it creates a feeling of order, maturity, and professionalism.

---

### F. Why Nordic Section

This should be a separate section, not mixed randomly into the page.

#### Purpose

To explain why someone should trust the company.

#### Structure

Use 3–5 concise reasons, such as:

- international outlook;
- structured execution;
- long-term partnership mindset;
- discreet and professional engagement;
- cross-border coordination capability.

#### UX requirement

This section must not be text-heavy.

Use short value points with clean spacing and disciplined visual hierarchy.

---

### G. Presence / Credibility Section

The site needs at least one block that gives the feeling of real business substance.

#### Possible formats

- regions / markets presence;
- international scope;
- project-oriented experience;
- partner-driven execution model.

#### Important rule

This section should create trust without disclosing sensitive clients, transactions, or operational details.

#### UX options

This can be done with:

- a simple world map;
- a regions list;
- minimalist trust indicators;
- an “international perspective / multi-market experience” layout.

---

### H. Contact Section

The contact section must be stronger and more intentional.

#### Must include

- clear invitation to connect;
- contact form or contact details;
- business email;
- optional location / region information;
- strong CTA framing.

#### Purpose

The whole website should lead naturally to this point.

#### Important rule

Even for a business card website, there must be a clear conversion path.

---

## 5. Required Page Logic / User Flow

The site should follow this exact logic:

1. **First impression**  
   Who this company is and what level it operates on

2. **Orientation**  
   Understand what the company broadly does

3. **Confidence building**  
   See that the company has structure, process, and credibility

4. **Differentiation**  
   Understand why this company is worth speaking to

5. **Action**  
   Encourage contact

### Recommended page flow

**Hero → About → Services / Capabilities → Approach → Why Nordic → Contact**

**Optional:** add a Presence / Credibility section before Contact.

---

## 6. Information Hierarchy Requirements

One of the biggest issues now is weak hierarchy.

The revised design must clearly distinguish:

- primary headline;
- section headlines;
- supporting copy;
- secondary information;
- CTA elements.

The website should not feel like everything has equal importance.

### Needed improvements

- stronger typographic hierarchy;
- more disciplined spacing;
- clearer visual rhythm between sections;
- stronger separation of content blocks.

The visitor must always understand:

- where they are;
- what is important;
- what comes next.

---

## 7. Design and Interface Requirements

### Visual direction

- premium corporate;
- clean;
- restrained;
- elegant;
- not flashy;
- not over-animated;
- not startup-trendy.

### Interface requirements

- strong whitespace;
- balanced typography;
- disciplined grid;
- consistent button styles;
- consistent section spacing;
- consistent card structure;
- refined mobile responsiveness.

### Avoid

- oversized abstract slogans;
- meaningless filler text blocks;
- decorative elements with no UX value;
- chaotic spacing;
- weak contrast between sections;
- empty sections that feel unfinished.

---

## 8. Navigation and Usability Requirements

The website must be easy to scan and easy to understand.

### Required

- each section must have a clear purpose;
- navigation labels must be intuitive;
- CTA buttons must be visible but not aggressive;
- the page should feel controlled and corporate;
- the mobile experience must preserve the same logic.

The website must not feel like:

> “beautiful but unclear”

It must feel like:

> “clear, modern, and professionally structured”

---

## 9. Content Design Rule

Even if the text remains general and discreet, each section must still communicate something real.

### Therefore

- no empty marketing phrases;
- no abstract repetition;
- no “innovation / excellence / future” filler without business meaning;
- no sections that exist only for decoration.

Each content block must answer a real question:

- Who are you?
- What do you do?
- How do you work?
- Why should I trust you?
- How do I contact you?

---

## 10. Tone of the Final Website

The site should feel:

- international;
- bank-friendly;
- partner-friendly;
- discreet;
- serious;
- operationally mature.

It should **not** feel like:

- a contractor trying too hard to sell;
- a defense catalog;
- a shell company with no substance.

---

## 11. Final Instruction to Designer / Developer

Please revise the website **not only visually, but structurally**.

The priority is to build a website with:

- clear logic;
- strong information hierarchy;
- better user flow;
- controlled corporate tone;
- enough substance to look credible;
- enough discretion to remain bank-safe.

The new version should combine:

- the modern visual quality of the current website;
- the clearer business structure and logic that the old website had.

### In simple terms

Keep the modern look of the new website, but rebuild the page architecture so it tells a clear and professional story from top to bottom.

---

## 12. Practical UX Summary for Implementation

### Primary goal

Build a corporate website that supports trust and first-contact conversion without exposing sensitive operational detail.

### UX principle

Every section must have a job:

- Hero = establish credibility
- About = explain the company
- Services / Capabilities = show substance
- Approach = show process
- Why Nordic = build trust
- Contact = convert interest into action

### Final benchmark

If a bank, partner, or first-time business contact visits the website, the impression should be:

**“This is a real, serious, well-structured company.”**
